Markets for Traders

Most trading education begins with signals and ends with performance charts. Professional trading begins elsewhere: with market mechanics, execution constraints, and the uncomfortable reality that being “right” is often irrelevant if a position cannot be carried, funded, or exited under stress.

Markets for Traders is the market-mechanics pillar of the Governance-First algorithmic trading curriculum. It is explicitly not about discovering alpha. Its purpose is to train practitioners to reason correctly about how trading systems actually behave once liquidity thins, correlations compress, funding tightens, and regimes shift. This pillar treats markets not as price series, but as machinery.

The Two-Book Sequence

The pillar is structured as a deliberate progression: conceptual literacy followed by forced execution realism. The books are designed to be read in sequence and revisited repeatedly as reference material.

Book I — The ABC of Trading

A mechanism-first introduction to market structure. Liquidity, carry, correlation, volatility, and funding are treated as equilibrium objects that determine what trades are feasible. The book is intentionally gentle in mathematics but strict in logic.

Book II — Markets for Traders

A hands-on tour de force. Ten Colab notebooks simulate explicit market mechanisms and force strategies to pay execution costs, face leverage limits, and fail under regime stress.

Execution Laboratories (Colab)

The notebooks are not demonstrations; they are laboratories. Their objective is not success, but exposure to failure modes that institutional traders encounter routinely.
