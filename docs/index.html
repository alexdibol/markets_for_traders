Markets for Traders
Mechanism-First Trading Architectures

This sub-collection is one of the three pillars of a broader professional training program for algorithmic trading. It is explicitly not about finding alpha. Its purpose is to train practitioners to design, reason about, and implement operationally sound trading architectures that remain coherent under execution costs, liquidity constraints, regime shifts, and funding stress.

No Alpha Claims Execution & Liquidity First Gap-Aware by Design Institutional Reality
Explicit premise: Markets are not price series. They are constraint-driven systems where liquidity, funding, execution, and regime dependence dominate realized outcomes.

The Two-Book Sequence

The pillar is designed to be read and used in order. The first book rebuilds intuition. The second forces execution. Neither book claims to close the gap between models and markets — that gap is named, revisited, and treated as a first-class object.

Book I — The ABC of Trading

A mechanism-first introduction to markets. Liquidity, carry, correlation, funding, and regime transitions are treated as tradable constraints, not background noise. No strategies. No optimization. Pure architectural literacy.

Book II — Markets for Traders

A hands-on execution laboratory. Ten fully specified Colab notebooks simulate market mechanisms and force the reader to confront execution costs, binding constraints, regime stress, and failure modes.

Read the book →
Design rule: Book I calibrates intuition. Book II recalibrates confidence. Neither promises deployable performance.
