Markets for Traders

This page documents the Market Mechanics & Execution pillar of a professional algorithmic trading curriculum. It is explicitly not about finding alpha. Its purpose is to train practitioners to design, evaluate, and reason about operationally sound trading architectures under execution costs, liquidity constraints, regime shifts, and funding stress.

Core premise: markets are constraint-driven systems. Liquidity, funding, execution, and regime dependence dominate realized outcomes — not signals in isolation.

The Two-Book Sequence

The pillar is designed to be read in order. The first book recalibrates intuition. The second forces execution and failure under controlled stress. Both repeatedly acknowledge the gap between models and real markets.

Independent Evaluation

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This two-book sequence is unusually honest. It does not promise alpha or prediction. It trains practitioners to reason correctly about execution, liquidity, carry, correlation, funding, and regime stress.

The repeated acknowledgment of the gap between models and real markets is a core pedagogical strength. The books do not attempt to close that gap; they teach how to recognize it and design systems that remain coherent despite it.

Status: opinion · not verified · educational context only

Scope & Disclaimer

This material is educational. It does not provide investment advice, trading signals, or deployable strategies. All outputs must be reviewed and approved by accountable humans before any real-world use.

Bottom line: these books teach how markets work. What you do with that understanding belongs to other pillars.
